You’ve just left a **design crit for Onboarding 2.0**. In 15 minutes you’ve got a **stakeholder review for Billing**, and straight after that, a **sync with Support on the new SLA feature**.

* **Billing revamp** → PRD, spreadsheets, finance feedback, edge case tickets
* **SLA feature** → Support macros, incident reports, PM spec, engineering estimates

### Without TabStax

You alt-tab like a maniac: “Where’s the billing PRD? What was the last comment from finance? Which dashboard shows churn by plan again?”
By the time the stakeholder review starts, you sound like you’re remembering, not leading.

### With TabStax

You’ve carved your world into three Stax:

* **“PM – Onboarding 2.0”**
* **“PM – Billing Revamp”**
* **“PM – SLA Feature”**

Each Stax has:

* The right docs and tools
* The dashboard already filtered
* A Start Page with:

  * 2–3 **Next Actions**
  * Bulleted **“Last decisions”** so you rehydrate your memory instantly

You finish the design crit, **close Onboarding Stax**, and hit **Open Stax → “PM – Billing Revamp”**.

* PRD opens
* Finance feedback doc is right there
* The last decision you wrote: *“Sarah (Finance) wants clear downgrade rules before sign-off”*
* Your Next Action: *“Open review by restating agreed constraints”*

You drop into the stakeholder review **already in their world**.

You’ve **shared each Stax with the relevant squad**:

* Onboarding Stax with design + growth
* Billing Stax with finance + eng

When you say “Let’s open the Billing Stax,” everyone is literally staring at the same context.
